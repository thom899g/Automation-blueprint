Beyond the Invoice: The Follow-up

Automating Reminders & Client Onboarding

A Course by Automation Blueprint

What Happens Next?

You've automated sending invoices, which is great! But what about the manual work that comes *after*?

  • Constantly checking your bank account to see if an invoice has been paid.
  • Sending awkward "Just checking in..." emails for late payments.
  • Manually creating a new project folder, Trello board, or Slack channel when a new client pays.
  • Writing a "Welcome Aboard!" email with all the project links.

Our Goal: Automate the Full Client Lifecycle

We will build two new workflows to handle these crucial next steps.

Automated Payment Reminders

If an invoice becomes overdue, automatically send a polite reminder email.

Automated Client Onboarding

When a new client pays, automatically set up their project space and send a welcome packet.

Part 1: Never Chase a Payment Again

Building an Automated Payment Reminder System

Step 1: The Trigger - A New Invoice is Sent

In Zapier, create a new Zap. The trigger will be your accounting software (e.g., QuickBooks) and the event will be "New Invoice".

Step 2: Add a "Delay Until" Step

We don't want to check for payment immediately. Add a step using Delay by Zapier. Choose the action "Delay Until" and map the Due Date from the invoice.

This tells the Zap to pause and wait until the invoice is actually due.

Step 3: Check the Invoice Status

After the delay, we need to see if the invoice has been paid. Add another action for QuickBooks and choose the event "Find Invoice". Use the ID from the first step to find the exact same invoice.

Step 4: Add a Filter

We only want to send a reminder if the invoice is overdue. Add a Filter by Zapier step with one rule: "Only continue if..." the Status from the "Find Invoice" step contains the word Overdue.

Step 5: Send the Reminder Email

If the filter condition is met, the Zap continues. Add a final action to send an email using Gmail or your preferred email service. Map the customer's email and invoice details into the body.

Part 2: The 5-Star Welcome

Automated Client Onboarding

From First Payment to Project Kickoff

Step 1: The Trigger - An Invoice is Paid

Create a new Zap. This one will trigger when an "Invoice is Paid" in QuickBooks. This signals a new client is ready to start.

Step 2: Create the Project Space

Add an action step. Choose your project tool (e.g., Trello) and the event "Create Board". You can even create it from a pre-made template board.

Step 3: Send the Welcome Packet

Add a final action to "Send Email" via Gmail. Welcome your new client and map the link to their new Trello board directly into the email.

Explaination of terms

This guide explains the key terms from our course on automating invoices and expenses. Use it to build a clear understanding of the tools and concepts involved.

Expense Processing

Email Parser

What it is: A tool that can "read" incoming emails, identify specific pieces of information based on a template you create, and extract that data for use in an automation.

Think of it like this: An email parser is like a smart assistant who reads your mail for you. You tell the assistant, "Every time a receipt arrives, find the total amount, the store name, and the date, and write them down on this list." The parser does exactly that, but for your digital emails.

Expense Report

What it is: A document that lists all the money spent for business purposes over a specific period. It's used for reimbursement and for tracking company spending.

Think of it like this: It's the official "shopping list" you show your company after a business trip. Our automated spreadsheet is essentially a real-time, digital version of this list.

Invoicing & Client Management

Invoice

What it is: A formal bill sent to a client after you've provided a product or service, detailing what they owe and how to pay.

Think of it like this: An invoice is the check a restaurant gives you at the end of a meal. It itemizes what you ordered and shows the final total you need to pay.

Accounting Software

What it is: A program (like QuickBooks, Wave, or Xero) that helps businesses track all their financial activity, from income and expenses to taxes and payroll.

Think of it like this: It's the official financial "brain" of your business. While a spreadsheet can track numbers, accounting software understands the rules of finance, making it essential for proper bookkeeping.

Client Onboarding

What it is: The process of welcoming a new client and getting them set up to work with you. This often includes creating project folders, sending welcome information, and scheduling kickoff meetings.

Think of it like this: Onboarding is like the orientation day for a new job. A good orientation makes a new employee feel welcome and gives them everything they need to succeed. A good onboarding process does the same for a new client.

Cash Flow

What it is: The net amount of cash moving into and out of a business. Positive cash flow means more money is coming in than going out.

Think of it like this: It's the financial "breathing" of your company. Automating invoice reminders helps you "breathe in" (get paid) faster and more consistently, which is crucial for a healthy business.
