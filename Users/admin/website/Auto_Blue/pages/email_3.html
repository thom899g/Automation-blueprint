The Fundamental Question

How does your email service (like ConvertKit, MailerLite, etc.) know when someone signs up on your website?

Your Website

Your Email List

By default, they don't talk to each other.

The Solution: The Integration "Bridge"

You need an extension or plugin that acts as a secure bridge for data.

Our Concrete Example

We will show how to do this with a very common and powerful setup:

Platform: WordPress

The world's most popular website builder.

Extension: Brave Popup Builder

A flexible WordPress plugin for creating forms and popups.

Email Service: ConvertKit

A popular email marketing platform. (The steps are similar for MailerLite, Mailchimp, etc.)

Step 1: Install the Plugin

In your WordPress dashboard, go to "Plugins" → "Add New" and search for "Brave Popup Builder". Then click "Install Now" and "Activate".

Brave Popup Builder

By Brave Apps

Step 2: Create Your Signup Form

Inside Brave, create a new Campaign. Drag and drop a "Form" element onto your canvas and add fields for Name and Email.

Newsletter Signup

Step 3: Get Your API Key

An API Key is a unique password that allows services to talk to each other. Log in to your email service (e.g., ConvertKit) and find it in your Account Settings.

ConvertKit Account > Settings > Advanced

Your secret key is used to authenticate with the API.

Step 4: Connect Brave to Your Email Service

Back in WordPress, go to Brave's "Integrations" settings. Select your email service from the list and paste in the API Key you just copied.

Brave > Integrations

Connect to ConvertKit

Step 5: Link Your Form to Your List

This is the final, crucial step. In your Brave form's settings, add an "Action". Tell it to send submissions to your email service and pick the exact list or tag.

Form Settings > Actions

After Submission...

How It All Works Together

  1. A visitor fills out the Brave form on your WordPress site.
  2. Brave's plugin captures the submission data (name, email).
  3. Using the API Key, Brave securely sends that data to your ConvertKit account.
  4. ConvertKit receives the data and adds the new person to your "Main Newsletter List".
  5. This new subscriber now triggers the "Welcome Email" automation you built!

This Concept is Universal

While we used a specific example, the principle is the same across all platforms:

You will always need a Form on your site and an Integration (via API key or a built-in app) to send the data to your email list.

This applies to Shopify apps, Squarespace blocks, Elementor forms, and more.

The Connection is Made!

You now have a system that automatically adds new subscribers from your website directly into your email funnels.

Explaination of terms

The worlds of marketing and technology have their own language. This can feel intimidating, but the concepts behind the terms are usually quite simple. Think of this guide as your personal translator, turning confusing jargon into clear, practical ideas.

The Bridge Between Your Tools

API Key (Application Programming Interface Key)

What it is: A unique, secret code that one application uses to identify and authenticate itself with another. It's a password, but for software instead of people.

Think of it like this: An API Key is like a highly secure, digital key card for a hotel. Your popup plugin (the guest) uses its specific key card to open the door to your email service's data (the hotel room). The hotelâ€™s front desk (the API) only grants access if the key card is valid.

Integration

What it is: The process of connecting two or more separate software applications so that they can automatically share data and work together.

Think of it like this: An integration is like hiring a professional translator. Your website speaks "WordPress," and your email service speaks "ConvertKit." The integration (like the Brave plugin) translates the "new signup" message, allowing them to have a seamless conversation.

Plugin / Extension

What it is: A smaller piece of software that adds a new feature or functionality to a larger existing program (like WordPress or a web browser).

Think of it like this: A plugin is like an attachment for a vacuum cleaner. The vacuum (your website) works fine on its own, but if you want to clean a specific spot (like create a popup), you attach a special tool (the plugin) to get the job done.

Growing Your Audience

Lead Magnet

What it is: A free, valuable piece of content or an offer you give someone in exchange for their email address.

Think of it like this: A lead magnet is like the free cheese samples at a grocery store. The store offers you a delicious, no-risk taste (the free checklist or ebook) in the hopes that you'll like it and decide to become a regular shopper (a subscriber).

Opt-in / Consent

What it is: The explicit, verifiable permission someone gives you to send them emails. This is a legal and ethical requirement (part of laws like GDPR and CAN-SPAM).

Think of it like this: Getting opt-in consent is like asking someone for their phone number before you call them. You wouldn't just find random numbers and start dialing. The person willingly gives you their number (email) and expects you to call (send emails).

The Language of Automation

Automation / Flow / Journey

What it is: A pre-built path of actions that are automatically triggered by a specific event. Different companies use different names, but they all mean the same thing.

Think of it like this: An automation is like a set of dominoes. You set them all up just once. Then, all you have to do is tip the first one (the trigger), and the rest will fall in sequence exactly as you planned.

Trigger

What it is: The starting event that kicks off an automation. The "if this..." part of an "if this, then that" statement.

Think of it like this: It's the starting pistol for a race. For example: *If a person signs up for my list...* (that's the trigger), *...then send them a welcome email.*

Action

What it is: A single step or task that is performed within an automation. The "...then that" part of the statement.

Think of it like this: It's what your system *does* after the trigger occurs. Common actions include "Send an email," "Wait for 3 days," or "Add a tag to the contact."

Inside Your Email

Call-to-Action (CTA)

What it is: The primary action you want the reader to take, usually presented as a button or a link.

Think of it like this: A CTA is like a clear, well-lit "EXIT" sign in a building. It removes all guesswork and directs people exactly where you want them to go next. An email without a clear CTA is like a hallway with no doors.

Transactional Email

What it is: An automated email sent to an individual in response to a specific action they took (e.g., password resets, shipping confirmations).

Think of it like this: It's like the receipt a cashier hands you after you buy something. It's not a marketing flyer; it's a functional confirmation of an action you just took, which is why these emails are opened so often.
