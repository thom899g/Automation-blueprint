Technical Terms Glossary

  • API Key (Application Programming Interface Key)

    A unique, secret code that one application uses to identify and authenticate itself with another. It's a password, but for software instead of people. Think of it like this: An API Key is like a highly secure, digital key card for a hotel. Your automation tool (the guest) uses its specific key card to open the door to your accounting software's data (the hotel room). The hotel‚Äôs front desk (the API) only grants access if the key card is valid.

  • Integration

    The process of connecting two or more separate software applications so that they can automatically share data and work together. Think of it like this: An integration is like hiring a professional translator. Your email speaks "Gmail," and your accounting software speaks "QuickBooks." The integration (like Zapier) translates the "new receipt" message, allowing them to have a seamless conversation.

  • Zap / Workflow

    A pre-built path of actions that are automatically triggered by a specific event in Zapier. Think of it like this: A Zap is like a set of dominoes. You set them all up just once. Then, all you have to do is tip the first one (the trigger), and the rest will fall in sequence exactly as you planned.

  • Trigger

    The starting event that kicks off an automation. The "if this..." part of an "if this, then that" statement. Think of it like this: It's the starting pistol for a race. For example: *If a receipt email arrives...* (that's the trigger), *...then log it as an expense.*

  • Action

    A single step or task that is performed within an automation. The "...then that" part of the statement. Think of it like this: It's what your system *does* after the trigger occurs. Common actions include "Create invoice," "Send email," or "Add row to spreadsheet."

  • Expense Report

    A document listing business expenses for reimbursement or tax purposes. Think of it like this: It's your "proof of purchase" collection for work-related spending.

  • Invoice

    A bill sent to clients for goods or services provided. Think of it like this: It's your "pay me please" note with details.

  • Cash Flow

    The movement of money in and out of your business. Think of it like this: It's the financial bloodstream keeping your business alive.

  • Client Onboarding

    The process of integrating new clients into your systems. Think of it like this: It's the welcome mat and guided tour for new business relationships.

  • Accounting Software

    Tools like QuickBooks for managing financial data. Think of it like this: It's the digital ledger book for modern businesses.
